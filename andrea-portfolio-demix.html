Demix — Atlas of the outskirts

Project Overview

Overview

This atlas, commissioned by the Italian Ministry of the Cultural Heritage, is a detailed analysis of cultural, commercial, social and environmental facilities in the outskirts of 9 major cities. I worked out some interactive tools to transform a large set of information into a series of visualisations to be published, and to enable researchers in performing their analyses.

Client

Mibact (Italian Ministry of the Cultural Heritage)
Project concept and scientific direction by Paolo Cottino (KCity), data management and GIS by Paolo Maneo (KCity), data collection by KCity team

The purpose of the project is to identify how different typologies of outskirts are located on the territory on the basis of the magnitude and mix of cultural, commercial, social and environmental facilities.

One of the goals is to compare the 9 cities, showing for each one of them the percentage of the outskirt area and the relative weight of each outskirt typology.

The interactive tools I developed allowed researchers to explore in deep the territory, selectively highlighting areas on the basis of the local weight of cultural, commercial, social and territorial indicators.

The outcome of the project was a print book, which should serve as a foundation for the planning of actions on the territory.

Some specific areas were investigated more in depth: the facilities in or closest to each area are represented on a map and additional infographics show magnitude and mix of the different variables.

In this overall visualization each city is represented with as many curves as the total amount of its outskirt areas; each curve is drawn connecting the values of all the indicators expressed in polar coordinates. With a proper key to interpret the diagrams, the spikes indicate which facilities are most lacking.
