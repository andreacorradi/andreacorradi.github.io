Audi drives Cortina

Project Overview

Overview

Looking forward to the next 2021 ski World Championship to be held in Cortina, Audi — as a main sponsor — wanted to mark its presence with a digital installation working during the four-year period preceding the event. The installation speaks about innovation, sustainability and quality of life.

Project designed and developed with the TODO team. Concept by Point architects, photo by Sirio Vanelli

There are 6 different isles, each one consisting in a bench, a totem led wall and a led bar matrix. 4 single isles feature the main topics (environmental noise, green energy, driving conditions and climate change) while 2 collective isles collect and display all data.

The 4 single isles display real-time data: the noise isle is equipped with a phonometer, the solar isle with a solar panel with a charge controller, the driving conditions isle with a visibility sensor and the weather isle collect data via API from the Arpa Veneto website.

The collective isles collect and display all data in the whole project timespan. They show how noise, solar energy, driving conditions and temperature change during days, weeks, months and seasons, and try telling if things are getting better or worse.

How to tell if things are stable, improving or worsening? Color codes tell if conditions tend to remain standard (white), better then standard (blue) or worse (red). In the collective isles, the size of the colored bubbles tell how much time the conditions have been in the three states.

A consistent narrative format has been designed for all the isles, presenting the topics to the public and giving people all the elements to read and understand the visualisations.

A matrix of led bars behind the led walls echoes the instantaneous value of the isle's variable shown on the led wall, using the same colors to classify it.
